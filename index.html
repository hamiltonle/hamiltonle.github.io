A 'Yelp' for Music Camps (2008)

geolocation, product mgmt, ui/ux, customer development, product roadmapping,...

In university, I didn't have much trouble finding music camps to work at, but that's because I enjoyed researching the various options. Those who didn't rarely found good learning opportunities for the summer. That's when I decided to put my bookmarks and notes online, which eventually become the "Summer Opportunities Database,"

It's interactive directory of Music Camps/Festivals around the world (mostly US though). I built it using an open map which could be fed an excel of geolcations. In addition to the directory, I sent out surveys to former camp attendees and had them rate camps (markers in orange have reviews), similar to a yelp model.

China Education Data from 1949-Present (2017)

web-scraping, OCR, tableau, data visualization, kaggle, data analysis

After talking to many EdTech enthusiasts in Shanghai who were always seeking more data-enriched resources, yet couldn't find any, I decided to go to the source and see what kind of data I could pull from the China government's own education website.

They had data from 1949-present on over 1000 different points, but the data were displayed as images. I then ran the images through an OCR, which I could then output into an excel.

I then used tableau to visualize some major trends, and later on will begin use D3.js to make more custom data visualizations to seek further insights.

Bandwagon: Easily Book a Band (2017)

rails, product mgmt, team mgmt, symmetrical model associations

This was created through a week long project to teach how Ruby on Rails works.

Teams were formed and we brainstormed ideas for an 'AirBnB of ____' idea. Since many team members were musicians, we agreed on a platform to book local bands in your city.

This rails app is a standard CRUD app, where you can sign-up/login, browse bands on an index page, see more details about a band on the band show page, use a form to enter dates and venue information for the band to see, then have the booking show up in your user dashboard. If you were both a user which booked bands and created bands to be booked, then your dashboard would show both.

For this project, I was the product manager, tasked with ensuring nobody was blocked on tasks, and at the end, giving a presentation of the platform.
